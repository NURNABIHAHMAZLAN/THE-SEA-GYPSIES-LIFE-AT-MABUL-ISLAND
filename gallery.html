Mabul Island in Malaysia is known for its sandy beaches and clear waters.
Located just about 15 minutes by speedboat from the popular Sipadan Island
It’s also a paradise for adventurous divers and is a great location for underwater photography.
Meet with the sea gypsies community there. The community that are unknown to the world.
Traditionally Bajau Laut are a boat-dwelling nomadic group that can be found on the Celebes Sea in the Sabah region of Borneo
The Bajau Laut lives on water of the Celebes Sea and Sulu Sea on boats or houses skillfully built in stilts
Sea gypsies have mastered free diving in order to make a living out of hunting for marine life that can be sold to the local.
Sea gypsies are capable of holding their breath and free diving to depth of over 20 meters without assistance
